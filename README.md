# Portico Webworks Plugin

**Requirements**

- WordPress 6.9.4 or higher
- PHP 8.3 or higher

## Important features

### Marketing features

**Promotional popups**
Highlight offers, seasonal deals, and F&B promotions with timed popups that activate and expire automatically — no manual updates needed. Trigger them on scroll, time delay, or exit intent, and target specific pages so the right message reaches the right visitor.

- **Exit intent** — Surface messages like "Book direct and save 10%" before a visitor leaves.
- **Seasonal offers** — Show a monsoon package only during the promotion window.
- **F&B promotions** — Display a weekend brunch promotion only on the dining page.

## Features

### Pages & content types

**Room type pages**
A dedicated page for each room category — Deluxe King, Executive Suite, Premier Twin, and every other room type you offer.

**Rooms listing page**
A single page that displays all your room types together, giving guests a complete overview before they choose.

**Restaurant & dining outlet pages**
Individual pages for each dining outlet — your all-day restaurant, rooftop bar, poolside café, or any other venue.

**Restaurants listing page**
A combined view of all dining options at your property, with cuisine type, meal periods, and a reservation call-to-action.

**Spa & wellness pages**
Dedicated pages for each spa outlet, featuring treatment highlights and a booking call-to-action.

**Spa listing page**
An overview of all spa and wellness offerings at your property.

**Meeting room & banquet hall pages**
Individual pages for each venue — with capacity, area, AV setup summary, and an enquiry call-to-action.

**Meetings & venues listing page**
A combined view of all meeting and event spaces, designed to help event planners shortlist and compare.

**Experience pages**
Dedicated pages for curated experiences — guided tours, cultural activities, adventure packages, and bespoke stays.

**Experiences listing page**
A browsable overview of everything your hotel offers beyond the room.

**Event pages**
Individual pages for specific events — galas, seasonal celebrations, weddings, and themed evenings.

**Events listing page**
A calendar-style or card-based view of all upcoming events at your property.

**Offer & package pages**
A dedicated page for each offer — advance purchase rates, honeymoon packages, staycation deals, and more.

**Offers listing page**
All active offers in one place, making it easy for guests to find the best deal for their trip.

**Nearby places pages**
Individual pages for places of interest near your hotel — landmarks, restaurants, beaches, and transit hubs — with distance and transport mode.

**Nearby places listing page**
A curated local area guide that helps guests plan their stay and positions your hotel as the expert on the destination.

**Custom static pages**
Add any page you need — photo gallery, wedding brochure, press kit — directly from WordPress, with no developer required.

### Search engine optimisation

**Clean, readable URLs**
Every page on your site gets a structured, human-readable URL — `/rooms/deluxe-king`, `/offers/early-bird` — the format Google rewards and guests trust.

**Automatic XML sitemap**
Your sitemap is generated and maintained automatically. Pages that are disabled are excluded from the sitemap without any manual action.

**Canonical URLs**
Every page declares its own canonical URL, preventing duplicate content issues caused by URL variations or query parameters.

**Open Graph tags**
All pages include Facebook and LinkedIn Open Graph metadata, so shared links display the correct title, description, and image every time.

**Twitter Card tags**
All pages include Twitter Card metadata for consistent, rich link previews when your pages are shared on X / Twitter.

**Schema.org structured markup**
Every page includes the appropriate schema type, helping Google display richer results — star ratings, event dates, offer prices — directly in search.

**Hotel schema** (`LodgingBusiness` → `Hotel`)
Applied to every property page to identify your hotel to search engines and enable knowledge panel eligibility.

**Room schema** (`HotelRoom`)
Applied to every room type page, enabling Google to display room-level information in search results.

**Restaurant schema** (`FoodEstablishment` → `Restaurant`)
Applied to dining outlet pages to surface cuisine type, opening hours, and reservation options in search.

**Spa schema** (`HealthAndBeautyBusiness` → `DaySpa`)
Applied to spa pages to identify wellness offerings to search engines.

**Event venue schema** (`EventVenue`)
Applied to meeting room and banquet hall pages to help corporate planners find your venue in search.

**Experience schema** (`TouristAttraction`)
Applied to experience pages to surface curated activities in Google's travel and discovery results.

**Event schema** (`Event`)
Applied to event pages with date, location, and price data — enabling rich results in Google Search and Google Events.

**Offer schema** (`Offer`)
Applied to offer and package pages, enabling price and validity information to appear in search results.

**Nearby places schema** (`TouristAttraction` / `LandmarksOrHistoricalBuildings`)
Applied to nearby places pages to strengthen your hotel's local relevance signals.

**Breadcrumb schema** (`BreadcrumbList`)
Applied to all pages, helping Google understand your site structure and display breadcrumb trails in search results.

**WebPage schema**
Applied to all pages as a base layer for structured data coverage.

**Organisation schema** (`Organization`)
Applied to the root of hotel group sites to identify the parent organisation to search engines.

**UTM parameter preservation**
All redirects on your site pass through campaign tracking parameters intact — your attribution data from email and paid campaigns is never lost.

**Disabled sections return clean 404s**
When a section is turned off, it returns a proper 404 response — not a misleading redirect — which is the SEO-correct behaviour.
